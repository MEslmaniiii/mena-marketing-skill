## What This Skill Teaches

This is not a knowledge base. It's a **production system**.

Every task in this repo has one job: put you in front of a real marketing problem and make you produce an output. No quizzes, no definitions, no theory sections. The learning happens in the doing.

The five tracks map to the five highest-leverage marketing skills in the MENA digital economy:

1. **Copywriting** — Words that sell. Arabic audiences respond to specificity, social proof, and directness. This track trains your instinct for what works.
2. **SEO** — Arabic search is underoptimized. The MENA market has massive organic opportunity most teams aren't capturing. This track teaches you to find and own it.
3. **Ads** — MENA has the highest mobile usage and social media penetration globally. Paid media here is both expensive and high-ROI if done right. This track gives you the reps.
4. **Analytics** — Most MENA marketers can run campaigns but can't read data. This track trains you to turn numbers into decisions.
5. **Growth** — GTM for a Saudi SaaS vs. an Egyptian e-commerce brand vs. a UAE service business are completely different problems. This track teaches the thinking, not a universal framework.

## Operating Principles

### 1. Output-First
Every task ends with a deliverable. Not "understand X." Produce X.

### 2. MENA-Specific Context
Avoid applying generic Western marketing playbooks. MENA has distinct dynamics:
- Ramadan marketing windows (traffic and behavior shift dramatically)
- KSA vs UAE vs EGY audience psychology differences
- Arabic vs English search behavior
- WhatsApp as a primary customer channel (not email)
- TikTok and Snapchat dominance over LinkedIn in most consumer categories
- Religious and cultural sensitivities that affect copy, imagery, and timing

### 3. Real Briefs Only
Tasks are written like actual client briefs. They give you:
- A product or service
- A target audience
- A channel
- A constraint or goal

You produce real output against real constraints.

### 4. Feedback Loop
After completing a task:
- Compare your output with the `examples/` folder
- Ask Claude to critique your output against the brief
- Note what you'd change and why
- Move to the next task — don't over-polish

### 5. Speed Over Perfection (in practice)
Marketers who ship 10 campaigns learn 10x more than marketers who polish one. Use these tasks to build speed and volume. Quality comes from repetition.

## MENA Market Quick Reference

### Key Platforms by Market
| Platform | KSA | UAE | EGY | Jordan/Levant |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | ✅ High | ✅ High | ✅ High | ✅ High |
| TikTok | ✅ Dominant | ✅ High | ✅ Dominant | ✅ Growing |
| Snapchat | ✅ Dominant | ✅ Medium | ❌ Low | ❌ Low |
| Twitter/X | ✅ Very High | ✅ Medium | ✅ Medium | ✅ Medium |
| LinkedIn | ✅ B2B only | ✅ B2B strong | ✅ B2B only | ✅ B2B only |
| WhatsApp | ✅ Primary CRM | ✅ Primary CRM | ✅ Primary CRM | ✅ Primary CRM |

### High-Traffic Seasons
- **Ramadan** (moveable): CPMs spike, emotional campaigns outperform rational ones
- **Eid Al-Fitr**: Purchase intent peaks 3 days before
- **National Days** (Sep 23 KSA, Dec 2 UAE): Brand plays, high engagement
- **Black Friday / White Friday**: KSA and UAE e-commerce peaks

### Arabic Copy Rules of Thumb
- Use Fusha for formal/professional content
- Use Egyptian dialect for mass consumer campaigns
- Use Gulf dialect for KSA/UAE-specific campaigns
- Never mix dialects randomly — it reads as inauthentic
- Avoid direct translation from English — rewrite for Arabic logic
