# Example Output: Product Headlines — Majer App
**Task Reference:** `tracks/copywriting/01-product-headline.md`

> This is a reference output — a strong starting point to compare against your own work, not a perfect answer.

---

## Context

**Product:** Majer — expense tracking app for Saudi professionals
**Audience:** Saudis 25–38, feel financial pressure at month-end

---

## Example Headlines

**Headline 1:**
`خلّص "وين راحت الفلوس" للأبد`
Format: Problem statement / command
Why it works: "وين راحت الفلوس" is something this audience says out loud — instant recognition. "خلّص" is confident and action-driven.

---

**Headline 2:**
`٣٧٪ من مصاريفك ممكن تختفي — لو عرفتها`
Format: Number-led + challenge
Why it works: A specific number builds credibility. The conditional "لو عرفتها" creates curiosity — it implies they're missing something.

---

**Headline 3:**
`بكام صرفت الأسبوع اللي فات؟`
Format: Question
Why it works: Most people genuinely don't know the answer. That moment of uncertainty is the hook.

---

**Headline 4:**
`تتحكم في مصاريفك قبل ما تتحكم فيك`
Format: Contrast / outcome
Why it works: The reversal names the real fear (losing control) without being dramatic.

---

**Headline 5:**
`دقيقتان كل يوم. وش ستصرف الشهر هذا — بدقة`
Format: Time investment + outcome
Why it works: "دقيقتان" minimizes commitment. "بدقة" signals reliability.

---

## Analysis Notes

**Strongest:** Headline 3 — the question exposes a specific knowledge gap the audience knows they have.

**Weakest:** Headline 5 — "بدقة" as a closer is weak. A stronger outcome word would be more resonant.

**What was avoided:** No "أفضل" or "مميز", no brand name opener, no vague quality claims.

---

## What to Compare in Your Own Output

- Do your headlines create personal recognition in the reader?
- Is your question headline specific enough that only this audience would answer "I don't know"?
- Do your number headlines use a specific-feeling number, not a round number?
