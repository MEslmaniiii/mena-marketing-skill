# Task: Ramadan Campaign — حملة رمضان
**Level:** ADVANCED | **Cross-Track:** Copywriting + Ads + Growth | **Time:** 2–3 hours

---

## The Brief

**Brand:** Tarbiya — تطبيق تعليمي للأطفال (3–12 سنة) باللغة العربية
Arabic-language educational app for children. Covers Quran memorization, Arabic letters, Islamic values, and stories. Subscription: SAR 79/month or SAR 599/year.

**Market:** Saudi Arabia (primary), UAE (secondary)
**Campaign Period:** 15 days before Ramadan through the 10th of Ramadan
**Campaign Goal:** 2,000 new annual subscriptions
**Budget:** SAR 85,000

---

## Why This Is Challenging

Ramadan is the most competitive marketing window in MENA. Every brand runs campaigns. To win:
- Your message must feel sincere, not opportunistic
- Ramadan for a children's education app has a real connection — this must come through
- Parents in Ramadan want to give their children meaningful content, not just screen time
- The best Ramadan campaigns create emotion first, conversion second

---

## Your Task — 3 Components

### Component 1: Campaign Strategy (400–500 words)

1. What is the campaign's core message? (Deeper than "download the app")
2. How does Tarbiya connect authentically to Ramadan?
3. Who exactly is the parent you're targeting? Describe them in a paragraph.
4. What are the 3 campaign phases? (Pre-Ramadan / First 10 days / Action phase)
5. What is the Ramadan-specific offer and why would a Saudi parent say yes?

---

### Component 2: Ad Copy — 2 Ads (Arabic)

Write 2 complete Meta ads targeting Saudi mothers, 28–42:

**Ad 1: Emotional / Brand**
This ad builds feeling. No hard CTA. Its job is to make a mother feel something about her child's Ramadan.

**Ad 2: Conversion / Offer**
This ad converts. Specific offer, specific urgency, specific CTA.

Format for each:
```
Primary Text (100–140 words, Arabic):
Headline (max 40 chars):
Description (max 30 chars):
CTA Button:
Creative direction (2–3 sentences):
```

---

### Component 3: Post-Campaign Retention Hook (200 words)

Ramadan ends. Many who subscribed for Ramadan will cancel. Write a plan for the 3 days before Eid to convert Ramadan subscribers into year-round retained users.

---

## Constraints

- Ad 1 (emotional) must NOT mention price or subscription
- Ad 2 (conversion) must mention the specific Ramadan offer
- Both ads in Arabic — Gulf/Saudi dialect preferred
- Strategy must acknowledge post-Ramadan churn risk

---

## After You're Done

Ask Claude: *"Play a Saudi mother of 2, aged 35, during Ramadan. I'm going to show you 2 ads. Tell me honestly what you feel when you read them."*
