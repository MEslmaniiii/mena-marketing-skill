## The MENA Analytics Gap

Most MENA marketing teams have access to data but don't use it effectively:

1. **Reporting, not analyzing** — "Traffic went up 12%" is a report. "Traffic went up 12% but conversions dropped 8% — here's why and what to do" is analysis.
2. **Vanity metrics** — Impressions, likes, and reach are not outcomes. Revenue, leads, and CAC are.
3. **No baseline** — Without a baseline, you can't know if anything is good or bad.
4. **Ignoring seasonality** — MENA data without Ramadan context is misleading.

## The Analyst's Mindset

Every time you look at data, ask 4 questions:

1. **What happened?** (The facts)
2. **Why did it happen?** (The diagnosis)
3. **What does it mean for the business?** (The impact)
4. **What should we do?** (The recommendation)

A good marketing analytics output is structured like a doctor's visit — symptoms, diagnosis, treatment — not just a temperature reading.
